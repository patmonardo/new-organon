   - **Status**: Needs chunking (lines 753-1207)

## 1. Definition (lines 1289-1648)

### Chunk 1.1: Introduction - Transformation to Concept Form (lines 1291-1312)
- Still given objectivity transformed into simple form (first form of concept)
- **"Still given objectivity"** = points to Objective Logic (Appearance, Thing)
- **Objective Logic is never self-determining** - concrete existence points to Appearance and Thing (Objective Reflections)
- Moments: universality, particularity, singularity
- Singular = object to be defined
- Universal = proximate genus = **universal with determinateness which is at the same time the principle for the differentiation of the particular**
- **NOT the Universal of the Pure Concept** - comes from Judgment (Subject/Predicate)
- Movement: Genus/Species → Subject/Predicate (Judgment) → Proximate Genus
- **Recursive structure**: Idea → Concept → Idea → Concept all the way down
- Particular = specific difference, determinate species
- **Keywords**: objectivity transformed to concept form, moments: universality/particularity/singularity, singular = object to be defined, universal = proximate genus, proximate genus = universal with determinateness that is principle for differentiation of particular, particular = specific difference

### Chunk 1.2: Definition Reduces to Concept (lines 1314-1337)

- **Keywords**: gets rid of externalities, abstracts from realization, reduces to simplest moments, subject matter = universal determined, singular = genus + particularization in one

### Chunk 1.3: Concept Finds Itself but Cognition is Subjective (lines 1339-1359)

### Chunk 1.6: Third Case - Concrete Objects (lines 1423-1471)
- Concrete objects (nature/spirit) = **things of many properties**
- **Properties are key for algorithmic analysis** (Entity-Property-Relation structure)

- **Universality from comparison** - with other concrete wholes (doesn't get beyond commonality)
- **Immediate properties** - single properties that constitute essence
- No criterion available than existence itself
- Universality is empirical (time or comparison)
- Would require theorems and proof
- **Keywords**: concrete objects = many properties, must determine genus/specific difference/essential, no criterion but existence itself, universality = empirical, would require theorems and proof

### Chunk 1.7: Definition Resorts to Marks (lines 1473-1500)
